Know your paper

What's different in 0264

0264 replaces 0450 from 2027. The format is almost identical — the emphases have shifted.

Paper 1 — Short Answer & Data Response

4 questions, 20 marks each = 80 total. Format per question: (a) 2m · (b) 2–4m · (c) 6m · (d) 8m. 1h 30min.

Paper 2 — Case Study

Pre-released case study with appendices. 4 questions: two (a) parts [8m each] and two (b) parts [12m each] = 80 total. 1h 30min.

Sustainability is now explicit

0264 directly examines how businesses respond to environmental pressures, carbon footprint, and sustainable development. It appeared in SP2 Q4 (eco-packaging + economic changes).

Updated terminology

"Statement of Financial Position" (not Balance Sheet). "Non-current assets/liabilities" (not fixed). Use 0264 language in every answer — examiners notice.

Higher AO3/AO4 weighting

Analysis and Evaluation carry more weight than in older 0450. The (d) 8-mark questions in P1 and the (b) 12-mark questions in P2 are almost entirely AO3+AO4. Most marks are not for knowing facts.

No past papers yet — only one official paper set

The official Paper 1 and Paper 2 are reserved for mock exams. Use the Practice Papers (P1 and P2) in this tool to prepare. The six 0450 papers are the same format — excellent preparation.

Paper 1 & Paper 2

Formulas & Ratios

Every formula you need. Always: write the formula → substitute the numbers → calculate → label the answer with units. Missing units or $ signs costs marks every series.

🚨 Universal calculation ruleAlways show: Formula → Substitution → Answer → Unit/label. The 2025 examiner reports flag missing $ signs, missing "million", and missing % signs as common mark-losers. Even if you get the number right, unlabelled answers may lose a mark.

Profitability

Gross Profit Margin
(Gross Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100
Gross Profit = Revenue − Cost of Sales. Shows % of revenue after direct production costs.
⚠ Don't subtract expenses — those come later for net profit margin.
Net Profit Margin (Profit Margin)
(Net Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100
Net Profit = Gross Profit − Expenses. Shows % of revenue remaining after ALL costs.
⚠ Always include × 100 and the % sign in your answer.
ROCE (Return on Capital Employed)
(Net Profit ÷ Capital Employed) × 100
Capital Employed = Total Assets − Current Liabilities. Compares profit to funds invested.
⚠ Compare ROCE to the interest rate — if ROCE < interest rate, it may not be worth investing.
Profit Markup
(Profit ÷ Cost) × 100
Markup is profit as % of COST. Margin is profit as % of REVENUE. Common confusion point.

Liquidity

Current Ratio
Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities
Expressed as X:1. Ideal = 1.5–2:1. Below 1 = cannot cover short-term debts. Above 2 = too much tied up in assets.
⚠ TT's 2025 current ratio = (8+1.5+2.5)÷(3+5) = 12÷8 = 1.5:1. Fell from 3:1 — a concern.
Acid Test Ratio
(Current Assets − Inventory) ÷ Current Liabilities
Removes inventory (least liquid asset). Ideal ≈ 1:1. Below 0.5 = serious liquidity risk.
⚠ TT's 2025 acid test = (1.5+2.5)÷8 = 4÷8 = 0.5:1. The $5m overdraft caused this drop.

Costs & Output

Average (Unit) Cost
Total Cost ÷ Output
Total Cost = Fixed Costs + Total Variable Costs. TT example: ($1000+$20000)÷5000 = $4.20
Variable Cost per Unit
Total Variable Costs ÷ Output
TT example: $20,000 ÷ 5,000 = $4.00 per dessert.
⚠ Variable cost per unit is NOT the same as average cost per unit.
Break-Even Output
Fixed Costs ÷ (Selling Price − Variable Cost per Unit)
The denominator is called "contribution per unit". Must be positive for the product to be viable.
Contribution per Unit
Selling Price − Variable Cost per Unit
Shows how much each unit sold contributes to covering fixed costs.
Total Contribution
Contribution per Unit × Output
Once total contribution exceeds fixed costs, the business makes a profit.
Margin of Safety
Actual Output − Break-Even Output
Shows how far output can fall before the business makes a loss. Express in units.
⚠ Nov 2025: Most candidates confused this with break-even. Learn the distinction.

Labour & Productivity

Labour Productivity
Output per period ÷ Number of employees
Always use ACTUAL output, NOT maximum capacity. SP1 Q3: 400,000 ÷ 80 = 5,000 units/employee. Do NOT use 440,000.
⚠ Using max capacity (440,000) instead of actual (400,000) = wrong answer = 0 marks for calculation.
Labour Turnover
(Number who left ÷ Average number employed) × 100
Expressed as a %. High turnover = recruitment costs, lost knowledge, disruption.

Finance

Gross Profit
Revenue − Cost of Sales
Cost of Sales = direct costs only (raw materials, direct labour). Expenses (rent, salaries) come later.
⚠ Expenses do NOT affect gross profit. This is one of the most common errors on Paper 1.
Net / Operating Profit
Gross Profit − Expenses
Expenses = rent, admin, salaries, marketing etc. Also called "profit" in most questions.
Working Capital
Current Assets − Current Liabilities
The funds available to pay day-to-day debts. Must be positive. Oct 24: 420−280 = $140m working capital.
Net Cash Flow
Cash Inflows − Cash Outflows
Closing balance = Opening balance + Net cash flow. SP1 Q2: X = 1200−1500 = (300). Y = 2000−500 = 1500.
June · March · November 2025

Examiner Warnings

These are real errors taken directly from the three 2025 Cambridge examiner reports. They repeat every series. Avoiding these is the fastest way to gain marks.

🚨 The #1 weakness — every series, every paperEvaluation (AO4) is the most failed skill. A decision alone is NOT evaluation. A summary of earlier points is NOT evaluation. You must: (1) make a clear decision, (2) give a reason based on your analysis, (3) explain why the alternative was the less good choice. Without all three, you stay at Level 2 in Paper 2 or get 0 eval marks in Paper 1.

Application errors

Using the same application twiceIf you use "chocolate" or "50 cafes" to support both of your points in the same question, only ONE gets the application mark. You need a different piece of case detail for each point. Any relevant reference from the scenario counts — it does not need to be complex.
Generic answers with no case referenceWriting about "a business" without linking to TT, HLC, or ZRE (whichever the question is about) loses all application marks. Name the business, its product, its number of employees, or use a figure from the table.
List questions: only the first answers are markedIf a question says "Identify four reasons..." only the FIRST FOUR answers are read. Write your best ones first. Don't add extras hoping for credit — they won't be read.

Analysis errors

Listing more points instead of developing the firstAnalysis = HOW or WHY using a cause-and-effect chain. Not "training improves quality AND increases efficiency." That's two knowledge points. You need: "Training improves quality [k] → fewer defective chocolate bars at the factory [app] → fewer customer returns → lower costs [an]." One developed chain beats three undeveloped points.
Repeating the same analysis for both points"This will increase sales/revenue/profit" said twice = credited once. Make the mechanism of each point genuinely different.

Critical concept confusions

Profit ≠ Cash FlowFlagged in ALL THREE 2025 reports. Cash flow = actual money moving in and out. Profit = revenue minus costs (can include credit). A profitable business can run out of cash. A cash flow forecast does NOT show profit or loss — it shows projected cash movements. Never say a business "can't pay its costs" because profits are low without checking the cash position.
Ethical ≠ LegalPaying the minimum wage is a LEGAL requirement — it is NOT ethical behaviour. Ethical behaviour goes beyond what the law requires: paying a living wage, ensuring fair supplier conditions, transparent marketing. Getting this wrong cost marks in June 2025 Q1(d) and November 2025 Q4(c).
Sustainable development ≠ sustainable production ≠ ethical behaviourSustainable development = meeting current needs without compromising future generations. Sustainable production = methods that reduce environmental harm. Ethical behaviour = morally correct conduct toward people. These three are related but distinctly different — using one when asked about another loses marks.
Dismissal ≠ RedundancyDismissal = the employee is terminated due to misconduct or poor performance (the JOB still exists). Redundancy = the JOB no longer exists, not the employee's fault. Getting these backwards in definition or scenario questions costs marks every series.
Quality Control ≠ Quality AssuranceQC = checking finished products at the END of production (reactive). QA = checks built into EVERY STAGE of production (proactive/preventative). Saying "inspectors check at the end" when asked about QA = wrong.
Lean production ≠ Flow productionLean = a philosophy of eliminating waste (includes JIT, Kaizen, zero defects). Flow = a continuous, high-volume production method. They are not the same thing. June 2025 flagged this as a "common problem."
Globalisation ≠ being a multinational companyGlobalisation = the process of increasing international economic integration and trade. Being an MNC = operating in multiple countries. You can benefit from globalisation without being an MNC. Confusing the two = lost knowledge marks.
Job rotation ≠ rest periods, and ≠ job enrichmentJob rotation = moving between different tasks at the SAME level to reduce monotony. Job enrichment = additional tasks with MORE responsibility/challenge. Job enlargement = more tasks at the SAME level. These are three different things.
Motivators ≠ Hygiene factors (Herzberg)Wages, working conditions, job security = hygiene factors (prevent dissatisfaction). Achievement, recognition, responsibility, advancement = motivators (actively drive performance). Wages are specifically NOT a motivator in Herzberg's model.

Calculation errors

Missing units and labels in calculationsMissing the $ sign, "million", or % sign costs marks even when the number is right. Always label what you've calculated: "Current Ratio = 1.5:1" not just "1.5". "GPM = 32%" not just "32".
Labour productivity: use actual output, not maximum capacitySP1 Q3 HLC: the answer is 400,000 ÷ 80 = 5,000 units/employee. Using 440,000 (maximum capacity) is explicitly flagged as wrong in the mark scheme. Zero marks for that error.
Cash flow calculation: opening and closing balance signsSP1 Q2 Oscar: X = 1200−1500 = (300) — the negative sign matters. Omitting the negative when the closing balance is negative costs a mark. Always show the brackets or minus sign.

Paper 2 specific (levelled questions)

What Level 3 actually requiresLevel 3 = excellent knowledge + detailed discussion of ALL options + well-justified recommendation that explains why the alternatives were rejected. If your conclusion only picks a winner without dismissing the alternatives, you're capped at Level 2.
Mirror arguments score once"Primary research is expensive" and "secondary research is cheap" = one credit, not two. They are the same point from opposite directions. Each point must make a genuinely different argument.
Promotion method: always name it specifically"Social media" = credit. "Online promotion" = no credit. "Television advertising" = credit. "Media" = no credit. Name the specific method. This rule applies across marketing questions every series.
Paper 1 (e) questions: how to structure evaluationState your decision → give a reason that BUILDS on your analysis (don't repeat it) → explain why the alternative option is weaker. Example: "On-the-job training is better because knowledgeable employees could increase sales [eval], offsetting the higher cost of off-the-job [eval], especially as ZRE operates in a competitive market [app]."

Q1 — FBM (Boat Building Business)

100 skilled employees · high inventory · diseconomies of scale · cost data
(a) [2m] Definition
Define 'diseconomies of scale'.
Your answer — 2 lines

When a business grows too large and average costs begin to RISE. Accept: when increases in output lead to higher average costs. Common causes: communication breakdown, poor coordination, low morale.

⚠ Do not confuse with economies of scale (lower average costs). Diseconomies = costs RISE.
(b) [2m] Identify
Identify two ways a business can use cost data to help make decisions.
Your answer — 2 lines

Set the selling price · decide whether a product is profitable · calculate break-even output · choose between suppliers · decide whether to continue or stop production · set budgets

(c) [4m] Identify
Identify four fixed costs a business might have.
Your answer — 5 lines

Rent · insurance · salaries (not wages) · interest payments on loans · depreciation · lease payments · rates/property taxes. Only first four answers marked.

⚠ Raw materials, electricity based on output, piece-rate wages = variable costs, NOT fixed costs.
(d) [6m] Explain
Explain one advantage and one disadvantage to FBM of holding a high level of inventory.
Your answer — 9 lines
K ×2APP ×2AN ×2

Advantage: Can meet unexpected increases in demand [K] for boats [APP] without delays → customer satisfaction maintained [AN]

Disadvantage: High storage / warehousing costs [K] for this boat building business [APP] → increases total fixed costs → reduces profit [AN]

(e) [6m] Justify
Do you think customers have a more important role in the success of a business than its employees? Justify your answer.
Your answer — 9 lines
K ×2AN ×2EVAL ×2

Either conclusion can score full marks. Must discuss BOTH customers and employees with analysis, then make a supported decision explaining why the alternative is less important. E.g.: Customers are more important because without revenue from sales, FBM cannot pay its 100 employees or cover its costs [EVAL]. Without customers there is no business. However, without skilled employees, boats cannot be built to the quality customers expect — but skilled employees can be replaced [EVAL].

Q2 — ADN (Food Retailer)

5 shops · 300 full-time employees · off-the-job training · tall hierarchy · autocratic leadership · internal vs external growth
(a) [2m] Define
Define 'span of control'.
Your answer — 2 lines

The number of subordinates/employees directly reporting to / managed by one manager. A wide span = many subordinates. A narrow span = few subordinates.

(b) [2m] Define
Define 'autocratic leadership style'.
Your answer — 2 lines

A leadership style where the manager makes all decisions without consulting employees / retains all authority. Employees are told what to do. Little/no employee input in decision-making.

(c) [4m] Outline
Outline two benefits to ADN of having full-time employees.
Your answer — 5 lines
K ×2APP ×2

More likely to be committed / loyal to ADN [K] less likely to leave the 5 shops [APP] · More consistent availability / reliable [K] meaning ADN's shops can maintain opening hours [APP] · Better understanding of ADN's products [K] improving customer service in the food retail shops [APP]

(d) [6m] Explain
Explain one benefit and one limitation to ADN of using off-the-job training.
Your answer — 9 lines

Benefit: Specialist trainers / expert knowledge [K] for ADN's employees [APP] → higher quality skills developed → improved customer service in food retail shops [AN]

Limitation: Employee is not working while training [K] → ADN must still pay wages + training cost [AN] → increases expenses for the 5 shops [APP]

⚠ Mirror arguments (benefit of off-the-job = disadvantage of on-the-job) are credited ONCE only.
(e) [6m] Justify
Do you think it is better for a business to use internal growth or external growth when planning to expand? Justify your answer.
Your answer — 9 lines
K ×2AN ×2EVAL ×2

Internal: Slower but lower risk · retain control · funded by retained profits · no culture clash
External: Faster growth · instant access to new markets · economies of scale · but risk of diseconomies · higher cost · possible culture clash

Evaluation: e.g. Internal growth is better for a food retailer like ADN because slower organic growth allows management to maintain quality across all 5 shops [EVAL]. External growth through takeover risks operational disruption and culture clashes which could damage ADN's reputation [EVAL].

Q3 — IDT (Clothes MNC)

Mass market · 4 countries · Statement of Financial Position · working capital · non-current liabilities
(a) [2m] Define
Define 'mass market'.
Your answer — 2 lines

A market where products are aimed at the widest possible range of consumers / large numbers of customers. Products are usually standardised and sold at competitive prices.

(b) [2m] Calculation
Calculate IDT's working capital in 2023.
Your answer — 2 lines

Working Capital = Current Assets − Current Liabilities = $420m − $280m = $140m
Method: 1 mark. Correct answer: 1 mark. Must include "m" or "million" for full credit.

(c) [4m] Outline
Outline one reason why IDT might need short-term finance and one reason why it might need long-term finance.
Your answer — 5 lines

Short-term: To pay wages / trade payables / cover cash flow shortfalls / buy raw materials for clothes production [K+APP]

Long-term: To buy machinery / build a new factory in one of 4 countries / fund major expansion [K+APP]

(d) [6m] Explain
Explain two ways an increase in non-current liabilities might affect IDT.
Your answer — 9 lines

Way 1: Higher interest payments on the increased debt [K] for this MNC operating in 4 countries [APP] → increases expenses → reduces net profit [AN]

Way 2: Greater financial risk / more difficult to repay debt [K] → may affect credit rating [AN] → harder for IDT to borrow further finance [AN] for its factories [APP]

(e) [6m] Justify
Explain two advantages to a business of being a multinational company. Which advantage do you think is likely to be the most important? Justify your answer.
Your answer — 9 lines
K ×2AN ×2EVAL ×2

Access to cheaper labour/materials [K] → lower unit costs → more competitive prices → increased sales [AN] · Access to larger/international markets [K] → spread risk across countries [AN] · Avoid trade barriers by producing locally

Evaluation: State which is most important + why + why the alternative is less important. E.g. Access to larger markets is most important because it increases revenue potential beyond the domestic market [EVAL], whereas cost savings from cheaper labour may be limited if quality must be maintained [EVAL].

Q4 — CFG (Social Enterprise / Chocolate)

Buys cocoa beans from local farmers · ethical · new chocolate bar · price skimming · retailers as distribution channel · legal controls on marketing
(a) [2m] Define
Define 'social enterprise'.
Your answer — 2 lines

A business that primarily aims to achieve social, environmental or community objectives / benefit society. Any profit is reinvested into the business or used to further social aims rather than maximising returns for owners.

(e) [6m] Justify
Do you think being ethical will always lead to lower profit for a business? Justify your answer.
Your answer — 9 lines
K ×2AN ×2EVAL ×2

Either argument can score full marks. E.g. No — ethical behaviour does NOT always lower profit. Paying fair wages to cocoa bean farmers [K] → better reputation [AN] → attracts ethical consumers who pay premium prices → increases revenue [AN]. This may offset higher costs. However, using more expensive ethical suppliers [K] → higher cost of sales → lower gross profit margin [AN] in the short term. Evaluation: Ethical behaviour may lower short-term profit but increase long-term profit through brand loyalty and premium pricing [EVAL]. CFG's social enterprise status means profit is not its primary objective anyway [EVAL].

⚠ Reminder: Ethical ≠ Legal. Paying minimum wage is legal. Paying a living wage is ethical. Do not confuse these.

NFC — Quality assurance definition
Your answer — 8 lines

FLD — Internal recruitment advantages
Your answer — 8 lines

Cheaper — no advertising or agency costs [K] for a business with 460 shops [APP] · Employee already knows the business culture/processes [K] → less induction time needed → maintains productivity across the 20,000 employees [APP+AN] · Motivating for existing employees — shows promotion opportunities [K] for FLD employees [APP]

TLA Hotel — Trade union definition
Your answer — 8 lines

An organisation that represents and protects the interests/rights of workers (employees). Must include: who is involved + purpose. "An organisation set up to help employees" alone = only 1 mark — too vague on purpose.

TLA Hotel — Diseconomies of scale
Your answer — 8 lines

Diseconomies = average costs RISE as a business grows TOO LARGE. Examples: communication breakdown across 3,000 employees → mistakes → higher costs [K+APP+AN] · Poor coordination between departments → inefficiency [K+AN] · Low morale from impersonal large organisation → higher absenteeism → reduced productivity [K+AN]

Practice Paper 1 · Question 1 · 20 marks

Vega — Soft Drinks Manufacturer

Secondary sector · Valencia, Spain · 120 employees · produces 600,000 bottles per week · sole trader considering incorporation · uses batch production · interest rates rising · sells to supermarkets and online

⚠ Examiner focus for this questionDefinitions must be precise — partial understanding earns 1 mark, but vague phrasing earns 0. In (b), each point needs a different piece of application. In (d), a decision alone is NOT evaluation — you must justify why one side outweighs the other.
1(a) · DEFINITION · [2 marks] · AO1 only
Define 'sole trader'.
Your answer — 2 lines
K ×2
  • Full marks (2): A business owned and run by one person, who keeps all the profits and has unlimited liability.
  • Partial (1): Some understanding, e.g. "a business owned by one person."
  • Zero: "A small business" or "a person who works alone" — too vague.
⚠ Do not write: Simply saying "one person owns it" without mentioning unlimited liability or keeps all profits = 1 mark only.
1(b) · OUTLINE · [4 marks] · AO1 + AO2
Outline two ways a rise in interest rates might affect Vega.
Your answer — 5 lines
K ×2APP ×2

Each way: 1 mark for identification [K] + 1 mark for relevant application [APP]. Different application required for each point.

  • Higher cost of borrowing [K] increasing Vega's loan repayments / interest payments [APP]
  • May reduce consumer spending [K] meaning fewer online orders for soft drinks [APP]
  • May reduce investment in new equipment [K] at the bottling factory / to increase the 600,000 bottles output [APP]
  • Supermarkets may reduce orders [K] if their own costs rise / demand for drinks falls [APP]
⚠ Do not: Use the same application reference (e.g. "120 employees") for both points — only one gets the APP mark.
1(c) · EXPLAIN · [6 marks] · AO1 + AO2 + AO3
Explain one advantage and one disadvantage for Vega of using batch production.
Your answer — 9 lines
K ×2APP ×2AN ×2
Advantages:
  • Flexible — can switch between different drinks varieties [K] allowing Vega to produce different flavours of soft drinks [APP] meeting changing consumer tastes and maintaining demand [AN]
  • Lower cost per unit than job production [K] when producing 600,000 bottles weekly [APP] improving profit margin [AN]
  • Machinery is not dedicated to one product [K] so Vega can respond to seasonal demand [APP] reducing the risk of idle equipment [AN]

⚠ Average (total) cost = Fixed costs + Total variable costs, divided by output. Using only variable costs = method error = 1 mark maximum.

variable cost = ___________
  • Correct answer: €4.50 per item [€18 000 ÷ 4 000 = €4.50] — 2 marks
  • Correct method but wrong answer: Total variable costs ÷ output — 1 mark
⚠ Variable cost per unit ≠ Average total cost per unit. Variable cost per unit excludes fixed costs. Know the distinction.

⚠ Sustainability ≠ Ethics ≠ Legal compliance. Using biodegradable packaging = sustainability/environmental practice. Paying fair wages = ethics. Complying with packaging laws = legal obligation. These are different concepts — using them interchangeably loses marks.

EXAMPLE LEVEL 3 CONCLUSION
The rise in interest rates is likely to have the greatest effect on Mira because it directly impacts both costs and demand simultaneously. Mira already has a €4m overdraft and may need a bank loan for the Barcelona expansion — higher interest payments will increase costs at a time when the business is already financially stretched, as shown by the acid test ratio falling to 0.5 : 1. The decrease in business profits tax is a positive change but it only affects profit after the business has earned it — it does not help with the immediate cash flow pressure caused by rising interest costs. The increase in income tax reduces consumer spending but its effect on ceramics demand is less direct than the impact of interest rates on Mira's own debt costs.
